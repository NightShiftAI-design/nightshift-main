How We Protect Your Data

NightShift AI is designed for hospitality operations where guest privacy, payment safety, and reliable escalation matter. We use practical safeguards and security-minded workflows to reduce risk and support dependable service.

Practices

Core security principles

These are the foundations we use to help protect configuration data, call metadata, and operational summaries.

Designed to Avoid Payment Cards

NightShift is designed to avoid collecting or storing guest credit/debit card numbers through AI voice agents. Payments can be routed through your existing secure processes (front desk, payment link, or your preferred workflow).

Secure Infrastructure

Systems run on secure cloud infrastructure with commonly used protections such as encrypted storage, network controls, and restricted system access.

Limited Access Controls

Only authorized staff and systems can access call logs and configuration data required to operate your service. Access is restricted to support setup, troubleshooting, and reliability.

Logging for Reliability

Call logs and summaries support operational follow-up, help detect issues, and improve escalation behavior. This helps reduce “missed context” when a human needs to step in.

ADA-Aware Handling Patterns

Call flows can be configured to follow ADA-aligned service animal handling patterns and reduce risk from inconsistent scripts. (Your policies still control the guest experience.)

Continuous Improvement

We continuously refine reliability, monitoring, and operational safeguards as the platform evolves. No system is 100% secure, but we aim to reduce risk with practical controls.

Clarity

What data is involved?

NightShift processes the minimum data needed to handle calls, route escalations, and provide summaries/logs for your staff.

Common operational data

Call time, duration, caller number (when available), call type/outcome, and structured reservation details (name/date/nights/room type) when provided by the caller.

What we avoid

NightShift is designed to avoid collecting guest payment card numbers over calls. If your workflow requires payment, we route to your secure process.
