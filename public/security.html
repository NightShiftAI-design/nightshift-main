Core security principles

These are the foundations we use to help protect configuration data, call metadata, and operational summaries.

What data is involved Contact

Designed to avoid payment cards

NightShift is designed to avoid collecting or storing guest credit/debit card numbers through AI voice agents. If your workflow requires payment, we route to your existing secure process (front desk, payment link, or your preferred method).

Secure infrastructure

Systems run on secure cloud infrastructure with commonly used protections such as encrypted storage, network controls, and restricted system access.

Limited access controls

Only authorized staff and systems can access call logs and configuration data required to operate your service. Access is restricted to support setup, troubleshooting, and reliability.

Logging for reliability

Call logs and summaries support operational follow-up, help detect issues, and improve escalation behavior. This helps reduce “missed context” when a human needs to step in.

ADA-aware handling patterns

Call flows can be configured to follow ADA-aligned service animal handling patterns and reduce risk from inconsistent scripts. Your policies still control the guest experience.

Continuous improvement

We continuously refine reliability, monitoring, and operational safeguards as the platform evolves. No system is 100% secure, but we aim to reduce risk with practical controls.

Security is operational

In hospitality, security isn’t only encryption — it’s making sure urgent calls escalate properly, workflows follow property rules, and sensitive information is minimized.

Does NightShift collect guest credit card numbers? Answer
NightShift is designed to avoid collecting or storing guest payment card numbers through AI voice agents. If payment is needed, workflows typically route to your secure process (front desk, payment link, or your preferred method).
Who can access call logs and configuration data? Answer
Access is limited to authorized staff and systems required to operate the Service, support setup, troubleshoot issues, and maintain reliability. Hotels control configuration choices and escalation contacts.
Are calls recorded or transcribed? Answer
Depending on configuration and local requirements, calls may be recorded or transcribed for quality, logging, operational follow-up, and reliability. Call handling laws vary by jurisdiction, and hotel customers are responsible for required disclosures/consent under applicable laws.
What happens during emergencies? Answer
Escalation can be configured to route urgent issues by rules (time, call type, property, role) so emergencies don’t get stuck. In true emergencies, callers can be instructed to dial 911, and your staff can be notified per your preferred procedures.
